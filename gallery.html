I am Reshma Sujani, the Founder and CEO of Girls Who Code, the international nonprofit organization working to close the gender gap in technology and change the image of what a computer programmer looks like and does. Mae C.Jemison

I am Mae C.Jemison, an astronaut and Physician. I was the first African American woman to be admitted into NASA’s astronaut training program. On September 12, 1992, I finally flew into space with six other astronauts aboard the Endeavour on mission STS47, becoming the first African American woman in space.

Merici Vinton

Hi! I’m Merici Vinton and I am one of the co-founders of Ada's List that launched in London in October 2013. Ada’s List aims to connect women in digital to one another, provide stimulating content that elevates individual commitment for change in the tech industry, and generally encourage a more inclusive and diverse environment in the industry. Women can join Ada’s List to recruit new teammates or job hunt themselves; look for suppliers or vendors; find co-founders; get advice; meet up face to face; people join Ada’s List to connect and act. Communities like Ada’s List keep women in the tech industry by providing off the record advice, peer to peer support, and mentoring when they need it the most.

Dr. Anita Borg

Dr. Anita Borg (1949–2003) combined technical expertise and fearless vision to inspire, motivate, and move women to embrace technology. Her dedication to creating change helped women see themselves as active participants in the realm technology. Anita received many honors for her important work in technology and advancing women in the field. She served on the National Research Council’s Committee on Women in Science and Engineering.
